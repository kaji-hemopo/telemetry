# Maintenance Tracking System v1.0
**Created:** 2026-03-23 23:25 JST  
**Purpose:** Track and manage all property maintenance, repairs, and preventative maintenance

## 🎯 SYSTEM OVERVIEW

### **Core Objectives:**
1. **Preventative Maintenance:** Schedule regular maintenance to prevent issues
2. **Reactive Repairs:** Track and manage repair requests from guests or cleaners
3. **Cost Control:** Monitor maintenance expenses against budget
4. **Vendor Management:** Track service providers and their performance
5. **Property Health:** Maintain property condition for guest satisfaction and asset value

### **Key Principles:**
- **Proactive over Reactive:** Prevent issues before they affect guests
- **Document Everything:** Complete records of all maintenance activities
- **Quality Focus:** Use reliable vendors for quality work
- **Cost-Effective:** Balance quality with cost control
- **Guest Experience:** Minimize disruption to guest stays

### **Maintenance Request Workflow:**
```

2. Triage → Assess urgency and category
3. Vendor Dispatch → Contact appropriate service provider

7. Follow-up → Check with guest if issue affected stay
```

## 💰 COST MANAGEMENT

### **Maintenance Budget:**
- **Monthly Allocation:** ¥5,000-¥10,000 per property
- **Annual Budget:** ¥60,000-¥120,000 per property
- **Reserve Fund:** 3-6 months of maintenance budget
- **Capital Reserve:** Additional for major replacements

### **Cost Control Strategies:**
1. **Preventative Maintenance:** Reduces costly emergency repairs
2. **Vendor Relationships:** Negotiate better rates for repeat business
3. **DIY Minor Repairs:** Simple fixes done by host if capable
4. **Warranty Claims:** Use manufacturer warranties when applicable
5. **Quality Purchases:** Buy quality items that last longer

### **Emergency Fund:**
- **Target:** ¥50,000-¥100,000 per property
- **Purpose:** Cover unexpected major repairs
- **Replenishment:** Fund from profits after use
- **Trigger:** Use for repairs >¥20,000 unexpectedly

## ⚠️ RISK MANAGEMENT

### **Common Maintenance Risks:**
1. **Guest Disruption:** Maintenance during guest stay
2. **Cost Overruns:** Unexpected high repair costs
3. **Vendor Reliability:** Poor quality work or no-shows
4. **Recurring Issues:** Same problem keeps happening
5. **Emergency Situations:** Urgent repairs needed

### **Mitigation Strategies:**
- **Scheduling:** Schedule non-urgent maintenance between guest stays
- **Multiple Vendors:** Have backup vendors for each category
- **Regular Inspections:** Catch issues early through preventative maintenance
- **Quality Materials:** Use durable materials and appliances
- **Clear Communication:** Set expectations with guests about maintenance

## 📞 EMERGENCY PROTOCOL

### **Emergency Definition:**
- **Life/Safety:** Fire, gas leak, electrical hazard
- **Property Damage:** Flood, broken window, lock failure
- **Guest Critical:** No heat in winter, no AC in summer, no water
- **Security:** Broken lock, unauthorized access

### **Emergency Response Steps:**
```
1. Assess Situation → Determine if truly emergency
2. Contact Guest → Inform of issue and plan
3. Dispatch Vendor → Use 24/7 emergency vendor
4. Authorize Work → Approve necessary repairs
5. Monitor Resolution → Stay in contact until resolved
6. Document Everything → Complete emergency record
7. Follow-up → Check on guest, review vendor performance
8. Review → Analyze emergency for prevention
```
