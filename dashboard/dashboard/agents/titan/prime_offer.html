FAQ

Common Questions

What if I've never worked with a PT before?
That's actually the best time to start. No bad habits to unlearn. I'll build your movement patterns from scratch, which is easier than fixing years of self-taught form. The trial session is designed specifically for this — it's a movement assessment first, training second.
Do I need a gym membership?
Yes — you'll need access to a gym with barbells, dumbbells, and a squat rack. Most apartment complexes in Tokyo have small gyms. Otherwise, any gym within 15 minutes of your home or office works. I can recommend gyms in Tokyo if you're new to the area.
What's your coaching style?
Direct. I tell you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear. If your form is wrong, I'll fix it. If your program isn't working, I'll tell you and change it. If you're not eating enough protein, you will hear about it. But I explain the why behind every decision — so you actually understand your body better over time.
Can I do this alongside a busy Tokyo work schedule?
Yes. Most of my clients are salarymen and women with 50-60 hour work weeks. Sessions are 60 minutes and I keep them efficient. Morning sessions (6-8am) or lunch sessions work for most people. I also program travel-safe sessions when you're overseas.
What's the commitment?
Monthly rolling — cancel anytime with 30 days notice. That said, meaningful strength changes take 3-6 months minimum. I'd recommend a 3-month commitment to see real results. The 12-month early-bird rate saves ¥192k if you're in it for the long term.
What if I get injured?
I screen for injury risk on day 1. If something comes up during training, we modify the programming immediately. I've managed multiple injury rehabs from my rugby days — I know the difference between "push through it" and "back off and let it heal." Your long-term health comes first.
Do you offer nutrition coaching?
Programming is included. Dedicated nutrition coaching is available separately at ¥30,000/month if you want it — meal plans, macro tracking, JP bento optimization. Most clients don't need it initially; getting the training right first is the priority.
What does the trial session involve?
60 minutes. Movement assessment (squats, hinge, push, pull, rotation) + first actual training block. I'll identify your biggest movement issues and give you specific cues to fix them. At the end, I'll tell you honestly whether I think PT with me is the right fit for you. No pressure.
